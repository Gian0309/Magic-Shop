Mental illness, also called mental health disorders, refers to a wide range of mental health conditions that affect your mood, thinking, and behavior. A mental health concern becomes a mental illness when ongoing signs and symptoms cause frequent stress and affect your ability to function. A mental illness can make you miserable and can cause problems in your daily life, such as at school, work, or in relationships.

Anxiety disorder is something that interferes with a person's daily activities. It refers to specific psychiatric disorders that involve extreme fear or worry, and includes generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder and panic attacks, agoraphobia, social anxiety disorder, selective mutism, separation anxiety, and specific phobias. Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) and posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) are closely related to anxiety disorders, which some may experience at the same time as depression. Anxiety is the mind and body's reaction to stressful, dangerous, or unfamiliar situations. It's the sense of uneasiness, distress, or dread you feel before a significant event. A certain level of anxiety helps us stay alert and aware, but for those suffering from an anxiety disorder, it feels far from normal and can be completely debilitating.

A mood disorder is a mental health problem that primarily affects a person’s emotional state. It is a disorder in which a person experiences long periods of extreme happiness, extreme sadness, or both. Mood disorders can cause changes in your behavior and can affect your ability to deal with routine activities, such as work or school. Depression is a common mental disorder and is a typical response to a traumatic life event or crisis however when it is still present even after the traumatic event then it is diagnosed as clinical depression. Symptoms must be present for at least two weeks and varies for every kind of depression. Bipolar disorder is defined by swings in mood from periods of depression to mania. Premenstrual dysphoric disorder occurs seven to 10 days before menstruation and goes away within a few days of the start of the menstrual period. It is believed that this disorder is brought about by the hormonal changes related to the menstrual cycle. Intermittent explosive anger is marked by unwarranted anger or, being angry for no reason.

Eating disorders are a range of psychological conditions that cause unhealthy eating habits to develop. They might start with an obsession with food, body weight, or body shape. It is common with adolescents but is not limited to that age group only. Anorexia Nervosa is the viewing one’s self as overweight even though they are severely underweight. They have the tendency to monitor their weight and avoid eating some food. Bulimia happens when people eat so much food until they are painfully full or, binging and continues to purge to make up for the amount of calories consumed. Purging includes vomiting, fasting, laxatives, diuretics, enemas, and excessive exercise. Binge eating disorder is eating unhealthy amounts of food in short periods of time. Those diagnosed with this do not have control over themselves during their episodes and do not purge after one. Pica involves eating food that aren’t supposed to be eaten. These foods aren’t included in their culture and are harmful to the body. Rumination disorder is when a person regurgitates food they have previously chewed and swallowed, re-chews it, and then either re-swallows it or spits it out. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder usually happens during childhood but can stay till adulthood. Those that have this could get disturbed and not eat their food due to certain characteristics.

A personality disorder is a way of thinking, feeling and behaving that deviates from the expectations of the culture, causes distress or problems functioning, and lasts over time.

  • A person with antisocial personality disorder may not conform to social norms, may repeatedly lie or deceive others, or may act impulsively.

  • Avoidant personality disorder is a pattern of extreme shyness, feelings of inadequacy and extreme sensitivity to criticism. They may be unwilling to get involved with people unless they are certain of being liked, be preoccupied with being criticized or rejected, or may view themselves as not being good enough or socially inept.

  • Borderline personality disorder is a pattern of instability in personal relationships, intense emotions, poor self-image and impulsivity. These people may go to great lengths to avoid being abandoned, have repeated suicide attempts, display inappropriate intense anger or have ongoing feelings of emptiness.

  • Dependent personality disorder is a pattern of needing to be taken care of and submissive and clingy behavior. Those that experience this may have difficulty making daily decisions without reassurance from others or may feel uncomfortable or helpless when alone because of fear of inability to take care of themselves.

  • Histrionic personality disorder is a pattern of excessive emotion and attention seeking. Some may be uncomfortable when they are not the center of attention, may use physical appearance to draw attention to themselves or have rapidly shifting or exaggerated emotions.

  • Narcissistic personality disorder is a pattern of need for admiration and lack of empathy for others. They would usually have a grandiose sense of self-importance, a sense of entitlement, take advantage of others or lack empathy.

  • Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder is a pattern of preoccupation with orderliness, perfection and control. Experiencing this may cause you to be overly focused on details or schedules, work excessively not allowing time for leisure or friends, or be inflexible in their morality and values.

  • Paranoid personality disorder is a pattern of being suspicious of others and seeing them as mean or spiteful. They would often assume people will harm or deceive them and don’t confide in others or become close to them.

  • Schizoid personality disorder is being detached from social relationships and expressing little emotion. They do not seek close relationships, chooses to be alone and seems to not care about praise or criticism from others.

  • Schizotypal personality disorder is a pattern of being very uncomfortable in close relationships, having distorted thinking and eccentric behavior. Those that experience this may have odd beliefs, peculiar behavior or speech or may have excessive social anxiety.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder is a series of reactions a person experiences after going through a traumatic event. This is triggered by either experiencing it or witnessing it. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares and severe anxiety, as well as uncontrollable thoughts about the event. Those that go through this may have difficulty coping and adjusting but with time and through self care, they may get better.

Psychotic disorders, when felt, affect the mind. It prevents the person from making good judgment, think clearly or communicate effectively. Those that experience this have trouble staying in touch with society.

Types:

  • Schizophrenic people have changes in behavior and other symptoms such as delusions and hallucinations that last longer than 6 months. It usually affects them at work or school, as well as their relationships.

  • Schizoaffective disorder, when experienced, have symptoms of both schizophrenia and a mood disorder, such as depression or bipolar disorder.

  • Schizophreniform disorder includes symptoms of schizophrenia, but the symptoms last for a shorter time, between 1 and 6 months.

  • Those that experience brief psychotic disorder have a sudden, short period of psychotic behavior, often in response to a very stressful event, such as a death in the family and usually recovers quickly.

  • The key symptom of a delusional disorder is having a delusion (a false, fixed belief) involving a real-life situation that could be true but isn't, such as being followed, being plotted against, or having a disease. This usually last for about a month.

  • Shared psychotic disorder happens when one person in a relationship experiences delusions and thir partner experiences it too.

  • Substance-induced psychotic disorder is caused by the use of or withdrawal from drugs, such as hallucinogens and crack cocaine that cause hallucinations, delusions, or confused speech.

  • Psychotic disorder due to another medical condition causes hallucinations, delusions, or other symptoms that may happen because of another illness that affects brain function, such as a head injury or brain tumor.

  • Paraphrenia has symptoms similar to schizophrenia. This is usually experienced by the elderly people.

  • Symptoms of these include hallucinations, delusions, disorganized or incoherent speech, confused thinking, slowed or unusual movements, loss of interest in personal hygiene, loss of interest in activities, problems at school or work and with relationships and mood swings or other mood symptoms, such as depression or mania.
